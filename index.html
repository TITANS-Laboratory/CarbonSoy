The Opportunity
A New Market Is Forming.
Most Farmers Don't Know Yet.

The 45Z Clean Fuel Production Tax Credit is changing how biofuel processors value soybean feedstock. Under this framework, lower Carbon Intensity scores mean higher tax credits; and processors will pay premiums for soybeans grown with documented climate-smart practices.

But the ISA February 2026 Researcher Roundtable found that most Illinois farmers have little to no understanding of what a CI score is, how it affects their operation, or whether changing practices is economically worthwhile. They specifically called for scenario tools that let farmers see how their CI score would change with different practices.

CarbonSoy fills this gap: combining an interactive CI scenario calculator with risk-adjusted economics and a blockchain verification prototype for the emerging 45Z supply chain.

Core Modules
Three Tools, One Platform
Each module addresses a distinct piece of the CI puzzle: from understanding your score, to evaluating the economics, to proving your practices.
📊
CI Scenario Calculator
Select your county. Enter your current practices: tillage, cover crops, nitrogen management, fertilizer timing. See your baseline CI score in gCO₂e/bushel. Toggle practices on and off to model how your score changes; individually or stacked. Built on USDA FD-CIC county-level data for every Illinois soybean-producing county.
💰
Risk-Adjusted Economics
Every CI scenario links to financial projections. See estimated per-bushel premiums, per-acre adoption costs, net ROI with confidence intervals, and payback timelines for your acreage. Not optimistic guesses: probability-weighted scenarios built by a commodity markets economist with real-world trading experience.
🔗
Blockchain Verification
The 45Z framework requires verifiable CI claims from farm to processor. CarbonSoy prototypes a lightweight blockchain ledger that creates tamper-resistant, auditable records of your CSA practice data; so when a biofuel plant asks for proof, you have a cryptographically verified answer. Built by a blockchain security researcher.
